s 211 of the code of criminal procedure relates to the contents of the charge. it inter alia provides that every charge under the code of criminal procedure shall state the offence with which the accused is charged. s 212 of the code of criminal procedure provides that the charge shall contain the particulars as to the time and place of the alleged offence and the person if any against whom or the thing if any in respect of which it was committed as are reasonably sufficient to give the accused notice of the matter with which he is charged. s 215 of the code of criminal procedure however clarifies that no error in stating either the offence or the particulars required to be stated in the charge and no omission to state the offence or those particulars shall be regarded at any stage of the case as material unless the accused was in fact misled by such error or omission and it has occasioned a failure of justice. s 464 of the code of criminal procedure relates to effect of omission to frame or absence of or error in charge.
